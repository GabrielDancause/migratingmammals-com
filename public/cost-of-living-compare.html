Frequently Asked Questions

Where does this data come from?

This tool uses a curated dataset of estimated monthly costs for popular digital nomad destinations. The figures are averages designed to represent a typical remote worker's lifestyle, including renting a 1-bedroom apartment in a central/popular area, eating out occasionally while also cooking at home, using public transit or renting a scooter, getting reliable home internet, and paying for a monthly hot-desk at a coworking space.

Are these costs for a local or a foreigner?

These estimates are tailored for foreigners, digital nomads, and expats. Locals often have significantly lower living costs as they may live outside popular expat hubs, share accommodation, or know the cheapest local spots. Nomads typically pay a premium for short-term rentals and international-standard amenities.

Why is coworking included separately from internet?

Many digital nomads require a reliable workspace outside their apartment. While home internet is essential for basics and backup, a coworking space provides a community, ergonomic seating, and guaranteed high-speed connections, which is a standard line item in many remote workers' budgets.

Does this include flights and visas?

No. This tool only compares the day-to-day cost of living once you are settled in the city. Flights, visas, travel insurance, and international health insurance are highly variable based on your nationality and origin, and should be budgeted separately.
