Frequently Asked Questions

Which credit cards have no foreign transaction fee?

Many travel and premium credit cards have eliminated foreign transaction fees. Popular examples include the Chase Sapphire Preferred, Capital One Venture X, Capital One Savor Cash Rewards, and the Platinum Card from American Express. Many no-annual-fee cards like the Wells Fargo Autograph and Capital One Quicksilver also offer this benefit.

Is the Chase Sapphire worth the annual fee for travel?

For most frequent travelers, yes. Both the Preferred ($95) and Reserve ($550) versions offer no foreign transaction fees, excellent travel insurance, and robust point-earning categories. If you travel internationally at least once a year, the benefits often outweigh the annual fee.

Do Capital One cards charge foreign transaction fees?

No, Capital One is unique among major issuers in that none of their US-issued credit cards charge foreign transaction fees, including their no-annual-fee cash back cards.

What is a foreign transaction fee?

A foreign transaction fee is a surcharge (usually 3%) applied by a credit card issuer on transactions processed outside the United States or in a foreign currency. Using a card with no foreign transaction fee can save you a significant amount of money while traveling abroad.

Which is the best credit card for international travel?

The "best" card depends on your travel habits. For luxury travelers, the Capital One Venture X or Amex Platinum offer excellent lounge access. For moderate travelers, the Chase Sapphire Preferred offers a great balance of rewards and insurance for a $95 fee. Budget travelers might prefer a no-annual-fee card like the Wells Fargo Autograph.
