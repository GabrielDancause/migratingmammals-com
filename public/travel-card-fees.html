Frequently Asked Questions

Which credit cards have no foreign transaction fee?
Many top travel cards offer 0% foreign transaction fees. Notable examples include the Chase Sapphire Preferred, Capital One Venture X, and the Wells Fargo Autograph Card.
Is the Chase Sapphire worth the annual fee for travel?
For frequent travelers, yes. The Chase Sapphire Preferred ($95 fee) offers excellent travel insurance and transfer partners. The Sapphire Reserve ($550 fee) includes airport lounge access and a $300 annual travel credit that offsets much of its cost.
Do Capital One cards charge foreign transaction fees?
No, Capital One is unique in that none of their consumer credit cards issued in the U.S. charge foreign transaction fees, making them excellent choices for international travel regardless of whether they have an annual fee.
What is a foreign transaction fee?
A foreign transaction fee is a surcharge (typically 1% to 3%) added to your credit card bill by the issuer when you make a purchase in a foreign currency or passing through a foreign bank.
Which is the best credit card for international travel?
It depends on your travel style. The Capital One Venture X and Chase Sapphire Reserve are top premium picks for lounge access. For no annual fee, the Wells Fargo Autograph and Capital One Savor are excellent choices.
